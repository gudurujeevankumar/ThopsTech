Software Requirements Specification

The SRS is not just documentation; it is the binding agreement between the business need and the engineering solution. It serves as the baseline for validation and verification.

The Cost of Ambiguity

CRITICAL

Why invest in a detailed SRS? The cost to fix an error increases exponentially as the project moves from requirements to maintenance.

"Fixing a requirements error during testing costs 15x more than fixing it during the design phase." - Boehm's Curve

Origins of Project Defects

ANALYSIS

Where do most software bugs originate? Surprisingly, logic errors in code are secondary to undefined or poorly defined requirements.

SRS Maturity Audit

Draft Professional

Correctness & Unambiguity

Every requirement must have exactly one interpretation. Avoid words like "user-friendly," "fast," or "typically." Use metrics.

Completeness

Include all significant requirements, responses to invalid inputs, and full labels/references for figures.

Verifiability (Testability)

If you can't write a test case for it, it's not a requirement. "The system shall be easy to use" is invalid. "The system shall require < 3 clicks to checkout" is valid.

Consistency

Internal logic must hold. Terminology must be consistent throughout the document.

The "Shall" vs. "Will" Rule

"Shall"

Indicates a mandatory requirement binding the developers.

"Will"

Indicates a statement of fact or future intent, not a requirement.

"Should"

Indicates a goal or non-mandatory recommendation.
